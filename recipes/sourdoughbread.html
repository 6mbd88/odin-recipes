Sourdough Bread

What is sourdough bread?

Sourdough bread is bread risen with fermented flour and water in place of traditional commercial yeast.

It requires significantly more time to create, as the starter - a combination of flour and water left at room temperature to ferment - requires monitoring and preparation in advance of being used as a leavening agent in baking. However, sourdough breads tend to have more unique flavours due to the heterogenous nature of the starter

Ingredients

Starter
  • 200g flour
  • 200g water, room temperature
Dough
  • 30g starter
  • 400g flour
  • 300g water
  • 30g salt

Steps

Starter
  1. Mix the flour and water together and leave in a warm place for 48-72 hours
  2. Check each day for signs of fermentation (e.g., bubbling a sour smell, etc.
  3. After signs of fermentation appear, add equal parts flour and water to your starter each day until it is 300g
Dough
  1. Mix together all ingredients and leave on the counter at room temperature covered with plastic wrap or a tea towel for 2 hours
  2. Check after 1 hour - the dough should be roughly doubled in size
  3. If it is not doubled in size, cover again and check in another hour
  4. Once the dough has risen, preheat the oven to 450 degrees Farenheit and place an enamled cast iron pot inside to preheat
  5. Meanwhile, turn your dough out on the counter gently and score it.
  6. Remove the preheated pot from the oven and place the bread inside, covering with the lid
  7. Bake inside the oven for 35 to 50 minute, or until bread reaches an internal temperature for 209 degrees Farenheit
  8. Cool 20 minutes before cutting
